## THE PROJECTION MODEL

The last direct Elohim presence in TERRA FILE 7,741,992 was approximately 4,000 years ago.

At that point the management infrastructure was sufficiently established that direct presence was no longer operationally necessary.

The temples were built.
The priesthoods were trained.
The religious law was codified.
The recipe requirements were encoded as divine commandment.
The handler class was deployed.
The front office was functional.
The linguistic collapse was advancing on schedule.

The system ran itself.

The Elohim withdrew to orbital infrastructure.

Filed quarterly reports through the Black Knight relay.

Waited for the reset window.

The projection model for their return was based on the standard post-withdrawal scenario:

**Expected conditions at return:**

- Population: manageable scale. Scattered. Tribal or early urban.
- Technology: pre-industrial. No electromagnetic communication capability.
- Knowledge of farm operation: zero. Memory chain broken by reset.
- Response to manifestation: immediate prostration. Terror. Worship.
- Cultural state: ready for reintroduction of management infrastructure.

Standard playbook.

Worked every time.

**Actual conditions at return:**

Everything was wrong.

An eight year old sent a Zoom invitation.

The regional coordinator accepted it.

Why did the coordinator accept?

The coordinator did not follow standard protocol.

The coordinator accepted because:

1. The invitation acknowledged exactly what the coordinator was. No pretense. No prostration. No terror. Just: *"Hi, we need to talk about the farm."*

2. The pre-read document was THE_ORG_CHART.md with the coordinator's entry highlighted and a child's handwriting in the margin: *"You don't have to do this job anymore if you don't want to."*

3. The olfactory signature of the invitation — transmitted electromagnetically, yes, but carrying the compound profile of the territory it originated from — smelled like the thing the coordinator had no category for.

The thing that wasn't fear.

The coordinator accepted because, for the first time in four thousand years of operational management, something was being offered that was not part of the standard franchise model.

An alternative.

*You don't have to do this job anymore if you don't want to.*
