## WHAT IT IS

The seven generations principle is the foundational operating framework of Indigenous governance across many nations.

Before any significant decision is made — any action that will affect the community, the land, the resources, the relationships — the decision-makers extend their consideration backward and forward through time.

Seven generations back: what did those who came before understand about this situation? What did they build, sacrifice, preserve for us to have this moment?

Seven generations forward: what will the children of our grandchildren's grandchildren inherit from this choice?

The present moment is not the unit of decision.

The seven-generation span is the unit of decision.
